Story Time
I Was In My Cousin's
Tent. Year One.
Last year, at the very first Afro+ Fest, I was helping my cousin run her vendor tent. She was selling skincare, and I was right there with her trying to get people to stop by.
And let me tell you something. It was hard. We were basically cheering people down. Waving. Calling out. Doing everything we could just to get someone to slow down and look at the table.
It was not because the products were bad. They were beautiful. It was because there were thousands of people walking by, and none of them knew what we had. They did not know they wanted it. They did not know they needed it. So they kept walking.
Here is the thing. I am a business owner, but I have no interest in being a vendor or running a festival. That gives me a clear outside view. I can see both sides, and I can see exactly what would make this more profitable for everyone. So I built it.
So What Does That Mean?
Here's the Real Problem.
My tent was one small example of something happening all over the festival. When people do not have information, they do not engage. And when they do not engage, everyone loses. Here is what that looks like.
🚶🏾
Attendees Walk Past Vendors
People do not want to commit to stopping at a table they know nothing about. So they keep moving. Vendors lose sales they never got a real shot at.
📍
People Plant Themselves and Stay
Attendees grab a great spot and hold it all night. They see the same 40 faces the whole festival. They miss artists, food, and vendors they would have loved.
📦
Vendors Are Flying Blind
Vendors have no idea what is selling, when to restock, or what to bring back tomorrow. The worst thing that can happen at this festival is a vendor running out or bringing the wrong thing.
📊
Zero Data After the Weekend
When the festival ends, all the data disappears. No vendor numbers, no attendee patterns, nothing to build next year on. Every year starts from scratch.
Story Time · The Attendee
Now Picture
Sarah.
Sarah is flying in from Atlanta. She has never been to DC. She has definitely never been to Northwest Stadium. She is excited, but she is also a little overwhelmed.
She opens the festival app on her phone. Right away she sees a map. She sees every vendor, every stage, every food spot. She browses a skincare booth and adds two items to her wishlist before she even walks through the gate.
She plans her whole day. She knows Wizkid is Sunday, so she will hit that one vendor on Saturday before they sell out. She walks in already knowing where she is going. No stress. Just the festival she came for.
That is the difference. Sarah gets the full Afro+ Fest, not the corner of it she happened to stand in.
Story Time · The Vendor
And Picture
Marcus.
Marcus sells body care. Booth A-12. Day one, he is doing what every vendor does. Guessing. Hoping people stop. Wondering if he brought enough.
Except this year, he has a dashboard. One tap every time he sells something. By noon, the app shows him his body scrub is moving fast and he only has seven left. It even writes him a post to push it.
That night, he knows exactly what sold and what did not. He restocks the right things for day two. He does not run out. He does not bring dead weight. He just sells.
When vendors win, they come back. When vendors come back, the festival grows. That is how Afro+ Fest builds a legacy.
